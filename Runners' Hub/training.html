__ HOW TO Train

Molly Seidel

Fartlek

Fartlek run is a type of training that involves varying your running pace and intensity by incorporating periods of faster running (sprints) and slower running (jogging or walking) in an unstructured and spontaneous manner. It's a versatile and flexible training method used to improve speed, endurance, and overall fitness.

Eliud Kipchoge

Long Slow Distance

Long Slow Distance (LSD) training helps runners improve their cardiovascular fitness, build a solid base of endurance, and enhance their ability to sustain a consistent pace over longer distances. It is a crucial component of many distance running training programs, especially for those preparing for events like marathons and half-marathons.

Jakob Ingebrigtsen

Threshold

Threshold running, also known as tempo running, offers a range of advantages to athletes, particularly those involved in endurance sports like distance running. This form of training involves running at a sustained, moderately high intensity close to one's lactate threshold. It provides several benefits, including an improved lactate threshold, enhanced endurance, better speed and stamina, the development of mental toughness, the ability to simulate race pace, efficient fat burning, time-efficient workouts, reduced risk of injuries due to its lower impact, and ultimately, better race performance. Incorporating threshold running into a structured training program or working with a coach can help individuals fully realize these benefits, tailored to their fitness level and specific athletic goals.

Faith Kipyegon

Tempo

A tempo run, also known as threshold running, is a workout where you maintain a challenging, but sustainable, pace for an extended period. It helps improve your endurance, speed, and race performance by getting your body used to running at a faster, steady pace. This type of training can also boost your aerobic capacity and mental toughness.
